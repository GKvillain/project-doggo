Nutrition

"Every dog deserves a healthy, happy life and it starts with proper nutrition. From understanding the basics of balanced diets to exploring homemade recipes, we’re here to guide you through every step of your dog’s wellness journey."

Dr.GK

" A specialist in bones and teeth plays a crucial role in maintaining skeletal and dental health. They provide expert guidance on nutrition, supplements, and lifestyle habits that strengthen bones, prevent fractures, and support healthy teeth and gums. Their advice is especially important for growing puppies, or breeds prone to joint issues, ensuring long-term mobility and overall well-being. "

Fat

The AAFCO minimum levels for fat in dog foods are 8.5% DM for growth and reproduction and 5.5% for adult maintenance.Fat has many roles in the body
Providing energy: Fat is the most concentrated form of energy
Helping with the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins

Minerals

Calcium (Ca) teeth and bones to maintain their shape
Phosphorus (P) cell growth, cell energy use, and amino acid and protein formation
Magnesium (Mg) metabolism of carbohydrates and fats
Potassium (K) maintaining acid-base balance and osmotic balance
