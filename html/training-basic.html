Dog Basic training

1

Train your dog to sit

Teaching your dog to sit is one of the easiest commands. Start by holding a treat close to their nose, then slowly move it upward and backward over their head. say “sit” and give the treat along with praise. Repeat consistently, and soon your dog will sit on command without needing a treat every time.

2

Train your dog to spin

Hold a treat near your dog's nose and slowly move it in a circle, guiding your dog to turn in a full circle. As your dog completes the spin, say “spin” and reward with the treat and praise. Repeat several times until your dog can spin on command without needing the treat as a guide.

3

Train your dog to come

Start in a quiet, distraction-free area. Say your dog's name followed by “come” in a happy, encouraging tone. When your dog comes to you, reward immediately with treats, praise, or play. Practice short distances first, then gradually increase distance and distractions until your dog reliably comes when called.

4

Train your dog to speak

Choose a cue, like “speak,” and get your dog excited with a toy or treat. When your dog barks naturally, immediately say “speak” and reward with a treat and praise. Repeat consistently, and soon your dog will bark on command. You can also teach “quiet” afterward to control barking.

Tips

Patience is key when training your dog, as every dog learns at a different pace. Avoid punishment or showing frustration, and focus on gentle guidance.

Make training enjoyable by turning exercises into fun games, rewarding successes, and celebrating small achievements, so both you and your dog look forward to each session.
