Expert Advice

Nutrition

Feed a balanced, high-quality diet appropriate for your dog's age, size, and breed. Avoid harmful foods like chocolate, grapes/raisins, onions, and cooked bones. Always provide fresh water.

Exercise

Dogs need daily physical and mental stimulation Mix walks, play, and puzzle toys to keep them engaged.

Safety & Comfort

Provide a safe space where your dog can relax.

Training & Behavior

Use positive reinforcement (praise, treats, play) instead of punishment.

Socialize your dog early with people, other dogs, and different environments. and be consistent with commands and rules.

Health

Keep vaccinations, prevention, and deworming up to date.

Regular Check-ups - Take your dog to the vet at least once a year for a full health examination. Older dogs may need visits every 6 months. and watch for red flags such as vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, excessive scratching, limping, sudden weight loss, or behavioral changes.

Caring for a dog means more than food and play it's about ensuring their long-term health, happiness, and safety.
